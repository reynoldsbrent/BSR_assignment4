Lisp

  • Creator: John McCarthy
  • Date Created: 1958

  • Lisp was created by John McCarthy in 1985 while at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. Lisp includes a family of programming languages such as Common Lisp, Scheme, and Clojure. Lisp is a functional and object-oriented language. Functions in Lisp are first class objects. Lisp is still used by many developers around the world and some large companies such as Grammarly and SISCOG. Lisp is used for web development, web scraping, data analysis and automation. In addition, Lisp is used in the Artificial Intelligence industry because of its ability to compute symbolic expressions.
